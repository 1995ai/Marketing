## Pain Points in Existing Apps

— App Store reviews ([verified](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/golf-bettor/id1506651354))

## Hammer Golf's Potential Differentiators

1. **PWA (no app store download, no account creation)** — Most competitors require native app install + account. BetCaddie is the only other "no account" option but it's offline-only.
2. **Splitwise-like simplicity** — Settlement is the actual pain point, not game tracking. Focus on "who owes who" resolution, not game library breadth.
3. **No install friction** — Share a link, everyone's in. Critical for the "convince 3 buddies to download an app" problem that kills golf app adoption.

---

## Market Context

- Golf at record participation: 47.2M participants, 82M rounds played
- Peer-to-peer score settlement is legally clean (friendly wagers, not prediction markets)
- The space got very crowded in 2024-2025 — differentiation is critical
- Feature competition is fierce — competing on game count is a losing strategy against WolfHammer (90+)

---

## Strategic Questions for Discussion

1. **Name collision:** WolfHammer Golf covers Wolf and Hammer games specifically. Does the "Hammer Golf" name create confusion?
2. **Feature vs. simplicity:** Do we compete on game breadth (losing battle) or settlement simplicity (open lane)?
3. **PWA advantage:** Is "no download" enough of a differentiator, or does App Store presence signal legitimacy to golfers?
4. **Pricing:** Most competitors are free or freemium. What's the monetization path?
